# PermitPath Research Findings: Pinellas County & Florida Bathroom Permits

## Key Research Questions & Answers

### 1. What Bathroom Work REQUIRES a Permit?

**PERMIT REQUIRED:**
- **Moving plumbing fixtures** (toilet, sink, shower/tub to new location)
- **Adding or relocating plumbing lines**
- **Adding new electrical circuits** or outlets
- **Moving electrical wiring**
- **Removing or altering walls** (especially load-bearing)
- **Changing bathroom layout/footprint**
- **Window or door changes** (size, location)
- **Major ventilation changes** (new exhaust fans requiring ductwork)
- **Shower pan installation** or drainage modifications
- **Water heater installation/relocation**
- **Structural changes** (opening walls, changing supports)

**Florida Residential Code References:**
- §P105.2: Requires permits for "alterations to water or drainage systems"
- §E3801.2: Requires permits for electrical alterations
- §R105.2: General building permit requirements

### 2. What Bathroom Work DOES NOT Require a Permit?

**NO PERMIT NEEDED (Cosmetic/Maintenance Work):**
- **Painting walls**
- **Replacing fixtures in SAME LOCATION** (toilet, sink, vanity - no plumbing connection changes)
- **Replacing faucets** (same location, no line modifications)
- **Installing new flooring** (without structural changes)
- **Re-caulking** around tubs/showers
- **Replacing light fixtures** (without new wiring)
- **Installing shelves, mirrors, towel bars**
- **Replacing vanity top/countertop** (same location)
- **Tile work** (cosmetic only - NOT if changing waterproofing/substrate)

**Important Caveat:** Pinellas County states permits required for work "in excess of $500.00 or which would require an inspection"

### 3. When Does Tile Work Trigger a Permit?

**Tile Exemption:**
- Floor tile/carpet installation = NO PERMIT (per Florida Building Code)
- Cosmetic retiling (surface only) = typically NO PERMIT

**Tile Requires Permit:**
- Changing shower/tub waterproofing systems
- Modifying substrate or drainage
- Shower pan installation or repair
- In humid areas with strict moisture protection codes (may flag shower/wall tile)

### 4. When Does Vanity Replacement Need a Permit?

**Vanity Exemption:**
- Replacing vanity in SAME LOCATION = NO PERMIT
- Same plumbing connections, no alterations = NO PERMIT

**Vanity Requires Permit:**
- Moving sink to new location
- Changing plumbing connections
- Adding/relocating electrical outlets
- Note: Miami-Dade/Naples specifically state vanity replacement "does require a permit" in some circumstances - Pinellas may vary

### 5. What Scope Triggers Electrical Permits?

**Electrical Permit REQUIRED:**
- Running new circuits
- Adding outlets or switches
- Relocating electrical boxes
- HVAC or ventilation electrical work
- Any work requiring cutting into walls for wiring

**Electrical Permit NOT REQUIRED:**
- Replacing light fixture (no new wiring)
- Replacing outlet cover plates
- Replacing fixtures in same location without circuit changes

**Code Reference:** NEC Article 210 (Branch Circuits), NEC 210.8 (GFCI requirements)

### 6. Dollar Threshold for Permits

**Pinellas County:** Work "in excess of $500.00" requires a permit
- This is a LOW threshold - most bathroom remodels exceed this
- Cost includes materials AND labor

### 7. Permit Fees in Pinellas County

**Typical Range:**
- Simple projects: $50-$100
- Moderate renovations: $200-$500
- Major reconfigurations: $500+
- Additional fees: Plan review, impact fees, inspection fees
- **Unpermitted work penalty:** Up to 100% of permit fee (DOUBLE the cost)

### 8. Required Inspections

**Bathroom Remodel Inspections:**
1. **Rough Plumbing** (before covering walls)
2. **Rough Electrical** (before covering walls)
3. **Framing/Structural** (if walls altered)
4. **Final Building Inspection**
5. **Final Plumbing Inspection**
6. **Final Electrical Inspection**

## Legal Sources & Code Citations

### Florida Residential Code (FRC) 2023 - 8th Edition

**Key Sections:**
- **§R105.2**: Work requiring permit (general building)
- **§P105.2**: Plumbing permit requirements
- **§E3801.2**: Electrical permit requirements
- **§M1301**: Mechanical permit requirements
- **§R303**: Ventilation requirements
- **§R307**: Toilet, bath, and shower spaces

### Florida Building Code (FBC)
- Chapter 1: Administrative provisions
- Permit exemptions listed in §105.2

### National Electrical Code (NEC)
- Article 210: Branch circuits
- §210.8: GFCI protection (bathrooms require GFCI)
- §210.52: Receptacle outlets required
- Article 422: Appliances (water heaters, fans)
- Article 625: EV charging (if adding circuits)

## Consequences of Unpermitted Work

### Legal Risks:
- Fines up to 100% of permit fee
- Stop-work orders
- Daily penalties until corrected
- Mandatory removal/reconstruction
- Property liens

### Financial Risks:
- Retroactive permits (double fees)
- Requirement to expose completed work
- Redo non-compliant work
- Difficulty selling home
- Buyer discounts/contingencies

### Insurance Risks:
- Unpermitted work may NOT be covered
- Claims denied for leaks, fires, damage from faulty work
- Personal liability for all damages

### Resale Risks:
- Buyers discover unpermitted work
- Mortgage lenders refuse financing
- Title insurance exclusions
- Home inspector red flags
