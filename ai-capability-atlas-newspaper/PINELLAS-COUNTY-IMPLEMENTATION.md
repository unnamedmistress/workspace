## ðŸ  Residential Permits

### Single-Department (Simple)
**Review Target:** 14 days  
**Cost:** $200-600  
**Examples:** Interior remodel (no structural), minor electrical, plumbing fixtures

### Multi-Department (Complex)
**Review Target:** 14 days  
**Cost:** $600-3,000+  
**Examples:** New construction, additions, structural changes

### Requirements for All Residential Permits:
1. **Two sets of plans** signed & sealed by Florida-registered architect/engineer
   - Alternative: Comply with Florida Building Code design criteria alternatives
2. **Engineering required for:**
   - All openings 16" or greater
   - Load-bearing wall modifications
   - Flood zones A/V (must show base flood elevation + 1 ft)
   - Construction on fill or unstable soil (soil report required)
3. **Energy Code Forms** (1 set)
4. **Product Approval List** (Florida approved products)
5. **Two surveys/site plans** showing existing & proposed structures
6. **Habitat Permit Form** (exterior work/new construction)
7. **Notice of Commencement (NOC)** if project value over $5,000

### Owner-Builder Rules:
- âœ… Can pull own permit for 1-2 family residence or farm outbuilding

## ðŸ“‹ Pinellas County Specific Requirements

### Flood Zones (A & V)
- **New construction:** Lowest floor = base flood elevation + 1 ft
- **Substantial improvements:** Same as new construction
- **Enclosed areas below base flood elevation:**
  - Cannot be partitioned or finished into rooms
  - Exception: Stairwells, ramps, elevators, storage â‰¤100 sq ft (max 6 ft dimension)
- **Elevation certificate required**
- **FEMA 50% Rule:** If improvement cost â‰¥50% of building value, entire structure must be brought to current flood standards

### Engineering Triggers (Pinellas County)
âœ… **Engineering REQUIRED for:**
- Openings 16" or greater
- Load-bearing wall removal/modification
- Construction on fill or unstable soil
- Flood zone construction (A, V)
- Decks over 30" height
- Structural beam/column work
- Second-story additions
- Foundation modifications

âŒ **Engineering NOT required for:**
- Express permits (like-for-like replacements)
- Cosmetic interior work
- Fence installation
- Minor plumbing/electrical

### Hurricane Zone Requirements (Florida-Specific)
- **High-velocity hurricane zone (HVHZ):** Miami-Dade & Broward County
- **Pinellas County:** Wind zone (not HVHZ)
- **Product approval required:** All exterior products must be on Florida Product Approval List
- **Truss engineering:** Pre-engineered trusses require sealed engineering on-site during inspection

## ðŸ”§ Finding a Licensed Engineer (Pinellas County)

### When You Need an Engineer:
1. **Structural changes:** Removing walls, adding beams, foundation work
2. **High/large decks:** Over 30" height or over 200 sq ft
3. **Flood zone construction:** A/V zones
4. **Solar installations:** Roof load calculations
5. **Unstable soil:** Fill, muck, or poor soil conditions
6. **Required by building department:** After plan review

### How to Find:
- **Search:** "structural engineer Pinellas County Florida"
- **Florida Engineering Society:** https://www.fleng.org/
- **Look for:** PE license (Professional Engineer), Florida registration
- **Experience:** Residential projects in coastal/hurricane zones

### Engineering Costs (Pinellas County Market):
- **Site visit + assessment:** $200-500
- **Simple beam calculations:** $500-1,000
- **Deck engineering:** $800-1,500
- **Roof load calculations (solar):** $800-2,000
- **Full structural plans (addition):** $1,500-3,000+
- **Flood zone analysis:** $500-1,500

### Timeline:
- Initial consultation: 1-3 days
- Engineering drawings: 1-3 weeks
- Revisions after review: 1-2 weeks
