## Domain 1: Knowledge & Learning
*Understanding, explaining, and acquiring new information*

### Category 1.1: Explain & Understand
*Making complex concepts accessible and comprehensible*

#### Capability 1.1.1: Explain complex concepts
- **What it is**: Breaking down complicated topics into understandable parts using analogies and simple language
- **When to use**: When learning something new, teaching others, or encountering unfamiliar terminology
- **Cognitive skills required**: Abstraction, Analogy formation, Simplification, Pattern recognition

##### Use Cases:
1. **Explain to a student**
   - *Description*: Making academic or technical concepts accessible to learners
   - **Example prompts**:
     - "Explain quantum computing to a high school student"
     - "What is blockchain in simple terms?"
     - "Break down how vaccines work step by step"

2. **Explain for decision making**
   - *Description*: Understanding complex information to make informed choices
   - **Example prompts**:
     - "Explain the pros and cons of different retirement account types"
     - "What are the key factors in choosing a mortgage?"
     - "Break down the arguments for and against universal basic income"

#### Capability 1.1.2: Answer factual questions
- **What it is**: Providing accurate information on specific topics
- **When to use**: When you need quick facts, definitions, or specific information
- **Cognitive skills required**: Information retrieval, Verification, Contextual understanding

##### Use Cases:
1. **Fact checking**
   - *Description*: Verifying information or getting accurate data
   - **Example prompts**:
     - "What's the population of Tokyo?"
     - "When was the Magna Carta signed?"
     - "What are the symptoms of Lyme disease?"

### Category 1.2: Research & Summarize
*Finding, analyzing, and condensing information*

#### Capability 1.2.1: Summarize long texts
- **What it is**: Condensing lengthy articles, reports, or documents into key points
- **When to use**: When you're short on time or need to extract essential information
- **Cognitive skills required**: Distillation, Prioritization, Synthesis

##### Use Cases:
1. **Summarize articles**
   - *Description*: Getting the gist of news articles or research papers
   - **Example prompts**:
     - "Summarize this 10-page article in 3 bullet points"
     - "What are the key takeaways from this research paper?"
     - "Give me a brief summary of this news story"

## Cognitive Skills Taxonomy

### Core Thinking Skills Identified:
1. **Abstraction** - Identifying essential patterns while ignoring irrelevant details
2. **Analogy formation** - Creating comparisons to explain unfamiliar concepts
3. **Simplification** - Reducing complexity while preserving meaning
4. **Pattern recognition** - Identifying recurring structures or relationships
5. **Information retrieval** - Locating and recalling relevant information
6. **Verification** - Checking accuracy and reliability
7. **Contextual understanding** - Grasping meaning within specific situations
8. **Distillation** - Extracting essential elements from complex information
9. **Prioritization** - Determining relative importance
10. **Synthesis** - Combining elements to form coherent wholes
11. **Divergent thinking** - Generating multiple possibilities
12. **Association** - Connecting related concepts
13. **Idea generation** - Creating new concepts or approaches
14. **Hierarchical thinking** - Organizing information into levels
15. **Sequencing** - Arranging elements in logical order
16. **Structure design** - Creating organized frameworks
17. **Precision** - Using exact and accurate language
18. **Conciseness** - Expressing ideas with minimal words
19. **Audience awareness** - Adapting communication for specific readers
20. **Criteria development** - Establishing standards for evaluation
21. **Comparative analysis** - Systematically comparing alternatives
22. **Trade-off evaluation** - Assessing costs and benefits of choices
