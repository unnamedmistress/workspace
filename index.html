The Problem

Diners face information asymmetry at restaurants. They cannot reliably assess nutritional content, allergen risks, or ingredient quality before ordering. Current solutions require manual lookup or guesswork, creating friction, health risks, and decision paralysis.

The challenge: Delivering accurate, contextualized food intelligence at the moment of decision without disrupting the dining experience.

Common Questions

Which concept is most viable?
Each targets different users: MenuLens for health-conscious millennials (broadest appeal), AllergenAlert for safety-critical use (highest willingness to pay), MacroMatch for fitness enthusiasts (existing tracking behavior). The MVP approach would start with one and expand.
How accurate is the nutrition data?
For chain restaurants, official data is 95-99% accurate. For independent restaurants, AI estimation combined with crowd verification achieves 85-90% accuracy. All concepts show confidence levels so users understand the reliability of each result.
Do restaurants need to participate?
No ‚Äî that's the key innovation. These solutions work anywhere via AI estimation, crowdsourcing, and computer vision. Restaurants can claim their listings for verified badges (B2B revenue), but user value doesn't depend on restaurant adoption.
What's the business model?
Freemium for viral growth: basic features free, premium subscriptions ($5-8/month) for advanced features, integrations, and family plans. B2B revenue from restaurant certifications and verified data programs.
Is allergen data safe to rely on?
For severe allergies, these tools provide informed questions to ask ‚Äî but always confirm with restaurant staff. Cross-contamination risks can't be detected through data alone. We always recommend confirming for severe allergies.
